Overview

Small businesses drowning in spreadsheets

Small retailers and warehouse managers often track stock using Excel files, WhatsApp messages, and paper notes — a fragile system that breaks at scale. This project aimed to design a mobile app that makes inventory tracking as simple as scanning a barcode.

The goal: reduce stock errors, surface low-inventory alerts in real time, and give business owners a clear picture of what they have, what's moving, and what needs reordering.

Diagnosis

User pain points, design responses

✕
Problems
  • Manual data entry is slow, error-prone, and hated by staff
  • No real-time visibility into stock levels across locations
  • Reorder decisions based on gut feeling, not data
  • Existing tools (ERPs) are over-engineered for small businesses
✓
Solutions
  • Barcode/QR scan-to-add — one tap to log items
  • Live dashboard with stock levels, movement trends, and alerts
  • Smart reorder suggestions based on historical consumption
  • Stripped-back UI — only the features small businesses actually need
Process

Research-driven, prototype-validated

01
User Research
Interviewed 8 small business owners to map pain points, current workflows, and must-have features.
02
User Flows
Mapped core journeys: add item, scan stock, check levels, set reorder alert. Optimized for minimum taps.
03
Wireframes & UI
Low-fi wireframes → usability testing → high-fidelity UI in Figma with a clean, data-forward aesthetic.
04
Prototype & Test
Interactive Figma prototype tested with 5 users. Iterated on navigation, scan flow, and alert settings based on feedback.
