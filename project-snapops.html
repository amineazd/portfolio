Overview

A tech product that needed a face

Snapops is a SaaS platform designed to streamline operations for small and mid-size businesses. The founders had the product — but no visual identity. They needed a brand that would feel modern, trustworthy, and tech-forward while standing out in a crowded SaaS market.

The challenge: build a brand system that works from a favicon to a billboard — flexible enough for UI components, marketing materials, and pitch decks.

Diagnosis

Brand needs, design system thinking

✕
Challenges
  • No existing brand — only a name and a product concept
  • SaaS market saturated with generic blue/gradient brands
  • Brand needed to work in product UI, not just marketing
  • Multiple stakeholders with different aesthetic preferences
✓
Design Direction
  • Orange/black palette — bold, energetic, differentiated from SaaS blue
  • Geometric logomark — scalable from 16px favicon to hero banners
  • Design tokens system — colors, spacing, and typography as variables
  • Component-ready: brand guidelines tied directly to UI kit patterns
Logo System

One mark, every context

The Snapops logo was designed to be instantly recognizable at any scale. The geometric mark references speed and connectivity — core product values. The system includes dark, light, monochrome, and accent background variants.
